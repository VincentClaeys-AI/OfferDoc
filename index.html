How it works

From first call to lasting habit

Every engagement follows the same structure. What changes across the three tiers is how much of it you get.

Before · Tier 2 & 3
Intake: I research your context
A short form. Industry, team, top three pain points. Ten minutes on your side. I use it to replace the generic demos with scenarios your team will recognise. This is what most trainers skip.
Session 1 · All tiers · 90 min
The core workshop
0 – 15 min Safety & governance. Your data stays in your tenant. The skeptics relax.
15 – 30 min GCSE prompt framework taught as a reusable skill. I show a bad prompt, fix it live, show what changes.
30 – 75 min Three live use cases: Outlook, Word (meeting transcripts), Excel. Real tasks, real failures, real fixes.
75 – 90 min Adoption close. One task, one win, one thing to try Monday. No homework. No recordings to watch.
Prompt doc in your inbox within 48 hours (Tier 2 & 3).
Week 3 · Tier 2 & 3
Session 2: specific scenarios and real blockers
90 minutes back with the same team. Now they've tried it. Now they have real questions. We work through specific scenarios for their role, clear the blockers, and go deeper on the use cases that matter most to them. This is where the framework becomes a habit.
After Session 2 · Tier 3 only
7-day challenge, then a prompt-a-thon
Daily prompt nudges for seven days keep the momentum going. Three weeks later, a live prompt-a-thon session: the whole team works through real tasks together, side by side, with me in the room. That's the point where casual users become consistent ones. All recordings and materials sit in a centralised hub they can access any time.
