What Copilot are we talking about

Three levels. Most companies are stuck on level one.

Not everyone means the same thing when they say "Copilot." There are three distinct versions. Each has very different capabilities.

Free
Copilot Chat
The free version. Browser-based. No connection to your Microsoft 365 data. Useful for standalone tasks. Not for working with company emails, documents, or meetings.
Licensed
Microsoft 365 Copilot
The one you've paid for. Integrated inside Teams, Outlook, Word, Excel, and PowerPoint. Reads your company data: emails, documents, meeting transcripts. This is the focus of every workshop.
Advanced
Copilot Agents
Multi-step workflows that run automatically across tools. Powerful, but most teams aren't there yet, and they don't need to be. I show what agents look like and when they make sense. The rule: if a good prompt solves it, we use the prompt. Agents come later.

Most employees who tried Copilot and gave up were using the free chat version, or using the licensed version like the free one. The workshop fixes that distinction immediately.

What changes and when

Three stages, one session

Stage 1
Permission to experiment

The first fifteen minutes kill the fear. Copilot is inside Microsoft 365. Your data doesn't go anywhere. You're not doing anything reckless. The room stops treating it like a liability and starts treating it like a tool. People go from crossed arms to leaning forward. You can feel the shift. It usually happens around minute twelve.

Stage 2
The first real output

I build a prompt live for a use case they actually deal with. Not a generic demo. Their job, their data, their workflow. I run it. We look at the output together. Then I build a second version to show what changes when one ingredient changes. The room stops being passive. Someone points at the screen: "Can you do that for my department?" That's the moment. The one that doesn't happen in any Microsoft webinar.

Stage 3
Monday morning capability

The session ends with something tangible: prompt sheets built for their department, a clear picture of what to try first, and no unanswered questions. They watched it work. They understand why it works. They know exactly what to type when they sit down Monday morning. Three weeks later, the ones who started using it are already showing others. That's how adoption actually spreads.

What to expect

What happens and when

This isn't a multi-week program. One session does the heavy lifting. Here's what the arc looks like:

Before the session (Tier 2 & 3)

A short intake form lands in your inbox. Industry, team, top three pain points. Takes ten minutes. That's what I use to build the cases. Generic sessions skip this. These don't.

The session (all tiers)

90 minutes online. Safety and governance first. Then the GCSE prompt framework. Then three live cases built for your context. I demonstrate in the tool, in real time, including what fails. Adoption close to finish.

Within 48 hours (Tier 2 & 3)

The full prompt doc lands in your inbox. Every prompt used during the session, formatted and reusable. No need to rewatch anything. Forward it to the team and it works on its own.

Days 1–7 after the session (Tier 3 only)

Seven short prompt assignments, one per day, tied to your team's real work. Not homework. A nudge. Each one takes under five minutes and reinforces something from the session before people forget it.

Week 3 (Tier 3 only)

A 60-minute Office Hours session. This is where adoption either sticks or quietly dies. Bring the blockers, the weird outputs, the "it didn't work for this one." I clear them live. After the call, I send a one-page recap: what came up, what it tells us, what to try next.
